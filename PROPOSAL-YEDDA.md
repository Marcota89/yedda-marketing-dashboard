## 2. Situation Analysis

### 2.1 The Current State of Mid-to-Large Operations in Retail and Logistics

Operations at your scale — whether in retail, quick-service food and beverage, logistics distribution, or manufacturing — share a common structural challenge: **the gap between what is happening on the floor and what leadership can see in real time is wide, expensive, and growing.**

CCTV infrastructure is near-universal. Most organisations with 50 or more locations have invested significantly in camera hardware. Yet in the overwhelming majority of cases, that infrastructure is used reactively — footage is reviewed after an incident is reported, not before a loss is incurred.

This creates three compounding problems:

**Problem 1: Shrinkage is systematically underreported and underaddressed.**
Industry benchmarks from the Retail Industry Leaders Association and equivalent logistics bodies in APAC and LATAM consistently show that internal shrinkage — employee theft, process error, and administrative fraud — accounts for 30 to 45% of total shrinkage losses. External theft accounts for a further 35 to 40%. Without real-time detection, the average time from incident to discovery is 3 to 6 weeks. By that point, the loss is unrecoverable.

In retail, shrinkage typically costs 1.5 to 2.0% of revenue. For an operation generating USD 100M annually, that is USD 1.5M to USD 2.0M in annual losses — before any consideration of logistics and warehouse shrinkage, which tends to run higher.

**Problem 2: Safety compliance is monitored sporadically, creating liability exposure.**
Manual safety audits are periodic. Violations — PPE non-compliance, unauthorised zone access, unsafe handling practices — occur between audits. The liability exposure from a single serious incident typically dwarfs the cost of continuous monitoring, yet continuous human monitoring at scale is prohibitively expensive.

**Problem 3: Throughput visibility is based on lagging indicators.**
Queue lengths, dwell times, loading dock utilisation, and picking efficiency are measured after the fact — through end-of-shift reports, manual counts, or ERP data that lags reality by hours or days. By the time a bottleneck is visible in your reporting, it has already cost you throughput, customer satisfaction, or carrier relationships.

### 2.2 The Opportunity

Your existing CCTV infrastructure represents a significant, underutilised asset. The cameras are already positioned. The network is already running. The footage is already being captured.

Yedda.ai's platform is designed to unlock that asset — converting passive video into structured, actionable intelligence — with no new hardware, no civil works, and no disruption to operations during deployment.

The opportunity is not to fix something broken. Your cameras work. The opportunity is to make them work for you — proactively, continuously, and at scale.

### 2.3 Competitive Landscape

The computer vision and AI monitoring space is growing rapidly across APAC and LATAM, driven by rising labour costs, tightening margins in retail and logistics, and regulatory pressure on workplace safety.

Several dynamics favour early movers:

- **Hardware-dependent competitors** require capital expenditure and long deployment timelines. Yedda.ai's software-only model eliminates both barriers, enabling faster deployment and faster ROI.
- **Point solutions** address one problem (e.g., queue management or access control) but do not provide unified operational intelligence. Yedda.ai provides a single platform across shrinkage, safety, throughput, and fraud.
- **Manual monitoring services** scale poorly and introduce their own consistency and compliance risks. AI monitoring is consistent, tireless, and auditable.

Organisations that deploy operational AI intelligence in the next 12 to 18 months will establish a meaningful structural advantage over competitors still relying on reactive, human-reviewed surveillance.
