## Campaign Strategy Overview

Yedda.ai sits at the intersection of two powerful enterprise buying triggers: the need to justify existing infrastructure investment and the mounting pressure to operationalize AI. The strategic advantage is clear — no new hardware means no capex conversation, which dramatically shortens the sales cycle with COOs and VPs of Operations who are perpetually under budget scrutiny.

The LinkedIn strategy is built around three phases. Phase 1 launches with a boosted version of the highest-performing organic post ("88% of companies say AI increased revenue. Only 64% are using it."), which already demonstrated resonance with the ROI-gap angle. This post is promoted as a Sponsored Content Single Image ad targeting the core ICP across APAC priority markets. Phase 2 introduces two additional original ad concepts — the passive surveillance angle and a hardware-free differentiator angle — structured as separate campaigns for audience learning. Phase 3 deploys a retargeting sequence for post-click and engagement audiences, pushing toward a demo booking.

Budget starts lean ($1,500–$2,500/month) given the organic-first mandate, scaling as signal accumulates from the first campaign. LinkedIn's CPCs in B2B SaaS targeting run $8–$18 USD, with lead form CPLs typically ranging $80–$160 in the operations/logistics decision-maker tier. All copy is written for a bilingual rollout — English for APAC, Portuguese/Spanish adaptations noted for LATAM.

## Retargeting Strategy

### Stage 1 — Awareness Retargeting (Warm: Ad Engagers, 60-day)

**Audience:** Users who liked, commented, shared, or clicked on Campaign 1 or 2 ads
**Goal:** Deepen brand familiarity, push toward product understanding
**Format:** Single Image Sponsored Content

- **RT-1A (Educational):** "How AI computer vision works in a retail environment — 3-minute explainer" → link to blog/video
- **RT-1B (Pain Point):** "If your CCTV isn't alerting you in real time, it's already behind" → link to use case page
- **RT-1C (Social Proof):** "Operations teams across APAC are activating their existing cameras with Yedda.ai" → link to case study

**Budget Allocation:** 35% of retargeting budget

---

### Stage 2 — Consideration Retargeting (Warm: Website Visitors 7–30 days)

**Audience:** Anyone who visited yedda.ai but did not convert
**Goal:** Overcome objections, demonstrate ROI specifics
**Format:** Carousel or Video

- **RT-2A (Case Study):** "How a [retail/QSR/logistics] operation reduced shrinkage by X% in 60 days" → case study landing page
- **RT-2B (Demo Teaser):** "See Yedda.ai on a live retail floor — 90-second walkthrough" → product demo video
- **RT-2C (Comparison):** "Why operations teams are choosing Yedda.ai over building in-house AI vision" → comparison page
- **RT-2D (FAQ):** "Your 3 biggest questions about AI vision for operations — answered honestly" → FAQ / blog page

**Budget Allocation:** 40% of retargeting budget

---

### Stage 3 — Conversion Retargeting (Hot: Lead Form Openers, Pricing/Demo Page Visitors)

**Audience:** Users who opened a Lead Gen form but did not submit, or visited high-intent pages
**Goal:** Drive demo booking or direct inquiry
**Format:** Single Image with Lead Gen Form

- **RT-3A (Offer):** "Free 30-minute operational assessment — we'll show you what your cameras are missing" → LGF
- **RT-3B (Urgency):** "Limited demo slots available this month for APAC operations teams" → LGF
- **RT-3C (Guarantee):** "No hardware. No commitment. Just a 20-minute look at what's possible." → LGF
- **RT-3D (Testimonial):** "'We went live in three weeks without touching a single camera.' — [Customer Title, Company Type]" → LGF

**Budget Allocation:** 25% of retargeting budget

## A/B Test Plan

### Test Priority Order
1. Audience segment (operations titles vs LP titles vs Plant Managers)
2. Offer (Free Assessment vs Book Demo vs See How It Works)
3. Creative concept (ROI gap vs surveillance angle vs hardware-free)
4. Headline wording
5. Lead Gen Form length (3 fields vs 6 fields)
6. CTA button copy

---

### Test 1: Creative Concept (Run First — Weeks 1–3)

**Hypothesis:** The ROI gap angle will outperform the surveillance angle for COO/VP-level audiences, while the surveillance angle will outperform for Loss Prevention titles.

| Variant | Creative Concept | Primary Audience |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| A | "88% / 64%" ROI Gap | COO, VP Operations |
| B | "Your cameras are watching" Surveillance | Director of Loss Prevention |

**Success Metric:** Cost per Lead Form Completion
**Decision Rule:** After 300 impressions per variant minimum (5 days), pause the higher-CPL variant
**Expected Winner:** Variant B for LP titles; Variant A for C-suite/VP titles

---

### Test 2: Offer / CTA (Run Weeks 3–5)

**Hypothesis:** "Free Operational Assessment" will generate higher form completion rates than "Book a Demo" because it lowers perceived commitment.

| Variant | CTA / Offer | Lead Form Headline |
|---------|------------|-------------------|
| A | "Book a 20-Minute Demo" | See Yedda.ai live on your operations |
| B | "Get a Free Assessment" | Find out what your cameras are missing — free |

**Success Metric:** Lead Form Completion Rate (%)
**Decision Rule:** 95% statistical confidence or 200 form opens per variant

---

### Test 3: Audience Segmentation (Run Weeks 4–7)

**Hypothesis:** Narrowing to 500+ employee companies will improve lead quality (demo-show rate) even if raw CPL is higher.

| Variant | Company Size Filter | Expected CPL | Expected Lead Quality |
|---------|--------------------|--------------|-----------------------|
| A | 201–10,000 employees (broad) | $70–$100 | Medium |
| B | 501–10,000 employees (narrow) | $90–$130 | High |

**Success Metric:** Demo-show rate (% of leads that attend a booked call)
**Note:** Requires CRM tracking to measure post-click quality

---

### Test 4: Lead Form Length (Run alongside Test 2)

| Variant | Fields | Fields List |

## Creative Brief for Designers

### Visual Identity Guidelines for LinkedIn Ads

**Tone:** Professional, data-forward, operationally confident. Not startup-flashy. Operations leaders respond to clarity, not hype.

**Color Direction:** Dark backgrounds (#0D1117 or deep navy) with high-contrast accent (electric teal or lime — confirm brand colors). White typography. Avoid stock-photo CCTV clichés (grainy green night vision).

**Typography:** Bold sans-serif for headlines (Inter, Neue Haas, or equivalent). Data numbers should be oversized and central. Do not bury the insight.
