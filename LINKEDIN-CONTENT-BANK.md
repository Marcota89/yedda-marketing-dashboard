### POST #22 — May 28, 2026
**Type:** Text Post | **Pillar:** Thought Leadership
**Topic:** Why "AI pilot" is the wrong framing — and what to ask for instead
**Source:** Yedda sales conversation patterns + NVIDIA State of AI 2026

---

**[COPY AND PASTE TO LINKEDIN]**

"We'd like to run a pilot."

I hear this in almost every first conversation.

And I understand why — AI pilots feel like risk management. A 90-day test before committing. A proof of concept before board approval.

But here's what most AI pilots actually are:

→ Scoped to one site, one use case, one team
→ Measured by outputs ("the system flagged X things") not outcomes ("we recovered $Y")
→ Ended at day 90 with a report that no one acts on
→ Designed to prove the technology works, not to prove it changes the operation

The pilot passes. The rollout stalls.

What actually works — and what we've seen produce ROI in real operations — is different:

✦ Start with a specific operational question, not a technology test
✦ Measure the cost of the current blind spot before you measure the system's output
✦ Set a week-one decision point: "What will we change in week two based on what we learn?"
✦ Design for operational adoption from day one, not demo success

The question isn't "does the AI work?"
The question is "does the operation change?"

If you're running an AI evaluation right now — what specific operational outcome are you measuring it against?
