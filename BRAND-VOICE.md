# Brand Voice Guidelines
## Yedda.ai
### Analysis Date: May 12, 2026

---

## Voice Summary

Yedda.ai speaks like a seasoned operations insider who happens to understand AI deeply — not a tech vendor pushing features, but a trusted advisor who has seen what breaks on the floor and knows what actually fixes it. The voice is direct and data-driven, always anchoring claims in specific numbers, and consistently addresses operators, directors, and VPs who are too busy for jargon but too experienced to accept vague promises. The overarching message is: "You already have the infrastructure. We unlock what it's telling you."

---

## Voice Dimensions

### Formal ←————————→ Casual: **4/10**

Leans slightly casual without sacrificing credibility. Uses contractions freely, speaks in first person ("At Yedda, we work with..."), and asks direct rhetorical questions to the reader. Never corporate-stiff.

**Evidence:**
- "Your cameras are watching. But are they actually working?" — punchy, conversational opener
- "Curious what that looks like in your operation? Drop a comment or send a DM" — informal CTA
- "Nobody was listening." — fragment sentence, deliberately casual
- "The question isn't 'should we use AI?' The question is..." — reframing in a conversational voice
- "Happy to share what we're seeing in the field." — peer-to-peer warmth

---

### Serious ←————————→ Playful: **3/10**

Firmly in serious territory. The problems being solved — shrinkage, safety incidents, workforce productivity — are high-stakes operational issues. The voice respects the weight of the decision. No humor, no wordplay. Occasional urgency ("that's the gap worth closing first") but never alarmist.

**Evidence:**
- "Retailers lose over $55 billion a year" — grounded in industry data
- "ROI of 180–400%. Payback in under 9 months." — precise, not hyperbolic
- "Safety flags before an incident becomes a liability" — serious consequence framing
- No exclamation marks used gratuitously; checkmarks (✦) used as functional bullets, not decoration

---

### Technical ←————————→ Simple: **6/10**

Slightly above midpoint toward simple. Uses domain terminology (CCTV, computer vision, throughput, shrinkage) without defining them — assumes an operational manager audience who knows the domain, not a general public audience. Avoids deep AI/ML jargon (no "neural networks," no "model inference").

**Evidence:**
- "Computer vision AI layered onto existing CCTV" — explains the tech in operational terms, not engineering terms
- "Turns passive footage into real-time operational intelligence" — translates tech into business value
- Never uses: RAG, embeddings, LLM, inference, transformer — deliberately absent
- Does use: shrinkage, throughput, compliance, workforce — domain-standard operations vocabulary

---

### Reserved ←————————→ Bold: **7.5/10**

Strongly bold. Makes specific claims without excessive hedging. Positions Yedda as the company that has already solved this problem for others and is offering a proven path. Directly challenges the status quo ("your surveillance isn't connected to action").

**Evidence:**
- "ROI of 180–400%." — no hedge, no "up to," no "typically"
- "Starting week one." — bold timeline promise
- "No new hardware. No re-architecture. Just answers." — three-part dismissal of standard objections
- "The companies winning with AI aren't the ones with the biggest budgets." — contrarian, opinionated

## Brand Personality

**Primary Archetype: The Authority**
Yedda speaks from a position of having done this before, in real environments, with measurable outcomes. It doesn't pitch possibilities — it reports results. The 54% fraud reduction, 55-second checkout wait reduction, and 3-year retail partnerships are offered as evidence, not promises.

**Secondary Archetype: The Guide**
Beneath the authority is a step-by-step logic: "Here's the problem. Here's why existing solutions haven't solved it. Here's the path. Here's what week one looks like." Yedda walks the prospect through the journey without overwhelming them.

**Archetype Fit: Strong** — Both archetypes are consistently maintained across all analyzed content.

## Vocabulary

### Words We Use (Frequently)

**Action words:**
- activate, unlock, turn (passive → active), connect, flag, close (a gap), reduce, integrate

**Descriptive words:**
- real-time, existing, live, operational, passive, proven, measurable, week one

**Value words:**
- intelligence, visibility, results, efficiency, safety, compliance, accuracy

**Operations domain:**
- shrinkage, throughput, workforce, CCTV, compliance, audit, incident, floor, dock, shift

**Framing patterns:**
- "not X — Y" (contrast structure): "Not at month-end — the moment it happens"
- Arrow bullets (→) for problem lists
- Checkmark bullets (✦) for solution lists

### Words We Avoid

- "revolutionary," "game-changing," "disrupting" — too startup-generic
- "leverage," "utilize," "synergize" — corporate filler
- "AI-powered" as standalone claim — always connected to a specific outcome
- "cutting-edge," "state-of-the-art" — unsubstantiated
- "solution" (standalone) — prefer "intelligence," "visibility," "answers"
- "we believe" / "we think" / "might" — hedging language

### Signature Phrases

- **"Turning your existing CCTV into operational intelligence"** — core product positioning
- **"No new hardware."** — recurring objection-crusher
- **"Starting week one."** — speed-to-value claim
- **"The gap worth closing"** — recurring framing for the space between surveillance and action
- **"What data do you already have that you're not acting on yet?"** — signature closing question

## Writing Guidelines

### Do's
- Lead with a sharp observation or statistic — not a feature or company name
- Use the contrast structure: "Not X. Y." to flip expectations
- Anchor every capability to a specific operational outcome (shrinkage, throughput, wait time)
- Use arrow bullets (→) for problems and checkmark bullets (✦) for solutions — it's a visual code readers have learned
- Address the reader as a peer with operational experience ("Most operations leaders I speak to...")
- End every post with a single low-pressure question or DM invitation
- Use specific numbers without ranges when you have them ("54%", "55 seconds", not "50–60%")
- List real geographies when proving scale (Malaysia, UAE, Germany)
- Keep paragraphs to 1–2 sentences maximum for LinkedIn format

### Don'ts
- Don't open with "We are excited to announce" or any company-first opener
- Don't use passive voice in headers or CTAs
- Don't make claims without a number, proof point, or named geography attached
- Don't use "AI" without connecting it to a specific operation or outcome
- Don't use adjectives like "powerful," "robust," or "seamless" without evidence
- Don't include more than 8 hashtags per post
- Don't end with a generic CTA ("Like if you agree") — always ask a specific operational question
- Don't mention competitors by name

## Recommendations

### Immediate Actions
1. Standardize all CTAs to "See How It Works" or "Book a 30-min Walkthrough" — remove generic "Learn More"
2. Add the boilerplate paragraph to the site footer and "About" section
3. Write posts 5–16 in the brand voice (they currently have only hooks and sources)

### Voice Evolution Opportunities
1. Introduce Abbey and Kai as named voices in content — "Abbey flagged this anomaly in 0.3 seconds. Here's what that means for your night shift."
2. Develop a "Field Notes" content series — short dispatches from real deployments (anonymized if needed) that feel like insider reports, not marketing
3. Expand the "IA philosophy" narrative: AI augments human judgment, not replaces it — this differentiates Yedda from fear-driven AI messaging

### Consistency Improvements
1. Audit all button labels across the site for voice alignment
2. Ensure case study format matches: [Outcome Stat] → [Context] → [How Yedda solved it] → [Result]
3. Create a one-page "voice cheat sheet" (from Do's/Don'ts above) for any new team member writing content
